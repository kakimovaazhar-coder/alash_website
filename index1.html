The Alash Movement

The Alash Party emerged in the early 20th century during the national liberation struggle of the Kazakh people. Its main goal was to create an independent Kazakh state. The collapse of the Russian Empire, caused by the First World War and the February and October Revolutions of 1917, created a political vacuum that encouraged the Kazakh national intelligentsia to defend the rights of the indigenous population.

Alash Orda Historical Regions

The roots of the movement began during the First Russian Revolution of 1905, when Kazakh representatives gathered in Uralsk to discuss forming a national party. These early efforts laid the foundation for the official creation of the Alash Party in 1917 at the First All-Kazakh Congress in Orenburg. The party arose in response to the need for national self-determination, protection of land rights, and the desire for political, cultural, and educational reforms for the Kazakh people.
