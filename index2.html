Alash Movement: 1917–1920

A historical journey of Kazakh national awakening

1917 – First All-Kazak Congress

Held in Orenburg, this congress discussed autonomy, political party formation, land reform, education, and courts. It led to the establishment of the Alash Party and the idea of territorial-national autonomy.

1917 – Draft Program Published

The "Kazakh" newspaper published the Alash Party program, outlining ten points: governance, justice, religion, education, land, and more — aiming to create a national Kazakh state with a presidential system.

1918 – Alash Orda Government Begins

Despite harsh conditions, the government started functioning in New Semey. Local bodies and departments like Turgai were formed. Courts and police institutions were established.

1918 – Goals and Reforms

Leaders promoted national unity, education, language institutions, and traditional agriculture. They opposed Russian peasant resettlement and pushed for modern socio-economic development.

1919 – Strategic Adaptation

As Bolsheviks gained control, A. Bukeikhanov encouraged younger members to work within the system to defend Kazakh national interests through the Bolshevik Party.

1920 – Integration and Influence

Figures like S. Sadvokasov and Y. Mustambayuly joined the Soviet government to protect national values. This was seen as a strategic move to preserve the Alash vision under new rule.
